# AI Ethics Statement

Principles governing how SITSOL builds and deploys AI features.

1. **Transparency.** Users are told when they are interacting with AI, not a human. AI involvement in deliverables is disclosed ([AI Disclosure](ai-disclosure.md)).
2. **Human accountability.** A human (me) is responsible for every system shipped. AI is a tool; it does not remove accountability for outcomes.
3. **Data minimization.** AI features send the minimum data needed to function. Sensitive data is excluded from prompts wherever feasible.
4. **No dark patterns.** AI conversational systems (e.g., lead-capture bots) identify themselves, honor opt-outs, and comply with applicable messaging regulations (e.g., A2P 10DLC, TCPA for SMS systems). `[ATTORNEY REVIEW for any outbound messaging product]`
5. **Bias awareness.** Where AI outputs affect people (lead scoring, content targeting), outputs are spot-checked for discriminatory patterns before and during deployment.
6. **Fitness boundaries.** AI systems are not deployed for decisions they are not fit for — no autonomous medical, legal, or credit determinations.
7. **Client informed consent.** SITSOL clients are informed when AI is part of a delivered solution, including its failure modes and operating costs.
